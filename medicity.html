Medicity - Medanta

Gurgaon, National Capital Region, India

Project Highlights

Monumental Scale

Spread across 43 acres, Medanta is a landmark institution with 2.1 million sq. ft. of construction, designed to accommodate 1250 beds, expandable to 1600.

Holistic & Healing Design

The design philosophy aimed to create an uplifting and happy space, moving away from traditional sterile hospital environments to promote healing.

Patient-Centric Planning

Each floor is dedicated to a specific specialty, creating a 'hospital within a hospital' to enhance efficiency and patient care. Wide corridors and ample natural light are prioritized. [12]

Advanced Technological Integration

The infrastructure integrates cutting-edge medical technology, including a BrainSUITE with intraoperative MRI and Da Vinci robotic systems.

International Standards

The facility is designed in compliance with top international codes, including the National Building Code of India, NFPA 101, and JCI standards.

Sustainable Elements

The building's white facade helps reflect heat, reducing the load on air conditioning systems, a key consideration for North India's climate.An Ayurvedic herbal garden is also included.

My Contributions

  • Conceptual Design & Spatial Planning: Played a pivotal role in translating the vision of a holistic healing environment into a functional architectural form, focusing on spatial hierarchy and the integration of natural light to enhance patient well-being.
  • Facade Development: Contributed to the design and detailing of the building's facade, ensuring it was not only aesthetically striking but also met performance criteria related to solar heat gain and maintenance.
  • Stakeholder & Code Coordination: Acted as a key point of contact for ensuring the architectural plans adhered to a complex matrix of international and national healthcare standards, including JCI and NFPA codes, facilitating a smooth approval and implementation process.
  • Interior Space Articulation: Involved in the design of key interior zones, ensuring that the material palette, lighting, and spatial flow contributed to a non-intimidating, patient-friendly atmosphere, from the grand lobby to specialized care units.
