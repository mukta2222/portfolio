Sangam Courtyard

Major Somnath Marg, Sector 9, R.K. Puram, New Delhi

Project Highlights

Urban Revitalization

Transformed an old single-screen cinema into a modern, vibrant commercial hub, rejuvenating the urban landscape of R.K. Puram. [8]

Complex Site Adaptation

Skillfully designed a functional and aesthetic structure on a challenging .83-acre curved site, successfully integrating two multiplex screens. [13]

Mixed-Use Integration

A successful fusion of entertainment and culinary experiences, hosting PVR Cinemas alongside a curated selection of premium cafes and restaurants. [4, 8]

Contemporary Facade Design

The original facade was replaced with a dynamic composition of glass, steel, and textured patterns, creating a modern architectural identity. [8]

Architectural Role

Design Conceptualization & Strategy

Led the initial design phase, focusing on the challenge of modernizing the iconic Sangam Theatre. My core responsibility was to develop a design that respected its legacy while introducing a contemporary mixed-use program, including the strategic placement of two cineplexes within the irregular site geometry.

Spatial Planning and Functional Integration

Orchestrated the spatial layout to create a seamless flow between the retail, dining, and cinema zones. This involved complex planning to ensure optimal circulation, patron experience, and the successful integration of diverse tenants like Starbucks, Delhi Club House, and PVR cinemas. [4, 6]

Material and Facade Innovation

Drove the selection of materials for the building's exterior, moving from the old plaster facade to a modern aesthetic using glass and pre-fabricated steel sheets. This decision was pivotal in creating the building's new, sophisticated identity and visual appeal in the neighborhood. [8]

Stakeholder & Engineering Coordination

Acted as a key point of contact between the client (PVR Limited) and various engineering teams. This role was crucial for translating the architectural vision into a viable structural and technical reality, ensuring the project was completed successfully within the set timeframe and budget. [17]
