House at May Fair Garden

New Delhi

Project Highlights

  • Contemporary Tropical Aesthetic: A design that masterfully blends clean, modern lines with lush, integrated greenery, creating a tranquil oasis within a dense urban context.
  • Indoor-Outdoor Symbiosis: The architectural plan emphasizes a seamless flow between interior and exterior spaces through expansive glazing, courtyards, and landscaped pockets.
  • Strategic Material Palette: A sophisticated use of natural materials, including rich wood cladding, textured stone walls, and expansive glass, achieving a warm yet minimalist character.
  • Biophilic Central Courtyard: A stunning double-height interior courtyard featuring a live tree under a skylight serves as the home's spiritual and visual anchor.
  • Volumetric Play and Light: The design features dynamic, interlocking cubist forms and cantilevered structures that create visual interest and manipulate natural light throughout the day.
  • Emphasis on Privacy: Cleverly designed facades and landscape elements provide substantial privacy from the street while maintaining an open and airy feel within the property.

My Contributions

  • Lead Design Conceptualization: Spearheaded the initial design concept, focusing on the integration of nature and the creation of hierarchical spatial experiences from the public to the private domains.
  • Material and Finishes Specification: Curated the selection of all architectural materials, ensuring a harmonious and high-end palette that enhanced the design's contextual and aesthetic goals.
  • Project Management & Coordination: Served as the primary point of contact between the client, contractors, and engineering consultants to ensure the faithful execution of the design vision.
  • Detail-Oriented Design Solutions: Developed innovative solutions for key architectural features, such as the internal courtyard structure and the bespoke facade details, ensuring both beauty and functionality.
