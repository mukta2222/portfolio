CIDADE DE GOA - V

Goa, India

Project Highlights

Heritage Modernization

A sensitive redevelopment and extension of the iconic 1982 hotel designed by master architect Charles Correa, preserving its unique Portuguese-inspired character. [2, 5]

Contextual Integration

The structure emerges organically from a sloping hill, creating a village-like atmosphere that respects the natural topography and overlooks the bay. [2, 4]

Seamless Indoor-Outdoor Flow

Designed with semi-open corridors, courtyards, and expansive decks to blur the boundary between built spaces and the lush tropical landscape. [3, 4]

Luxury & Convention Hub

The new extension adds a modern 5-star hotel and one of the largest convention facilities in Goa, redefining luxury and business tourism in the region. [3, 5]

Architectural Role

  • Led the design development for the new extension wing, ensuring architectural synergy with Charles Correa's original masterpiece while introducing contemporary aesthetics and functionality.
  • Oversaw the master planning and integration of the new convention center and hotel tower, focusing on circulation, spatial hierarchy, and optimizing coastal views.
  • Developed and detailed the facade systems, including high-performance glazing and solar shading elements, to achieve environmental efficiency and guest comfort.
  • Coordinated with a multidisciplinary team of engineers, landscape architects, and interior designers to ensure the project's design integrity was executed flawlessly from concept to completion.
