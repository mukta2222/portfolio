Uniworld City, Mixed Use

Rajarhat, Kolkata

Project Highlights

Integrated Township

A sprawling 100-acre master-planned development combining high-rise residential towers, commercial spaces, and retail zones to create a self-sustained urban environment. [6, 14]

Contemporary Architecture

The design merges international contemporary aesthetics with regional identity, utilizing local materials and responding to Kolkata's specific climatic conditions. [3]

Phased Development

The township includes multiple distinct residential phases such as Air, Harmony, and Heights, each with unique characteristics and architectural expressions. [6, 10]

Green Urbanism

Features generously scaled urban parks, a central axis of plazas with hardscapes and water features, and a meandering green floral band weaving through the entire development. [3]

My Contributions

  • Facade Design Leadership: Spearheaded the facade design for the landmark AIR (G+40 Towers), Retail and Club, focusing on creating a visually striking and climatically responsive building envelope.
  • Design Conceptualization & Execution: Played a pivotal role in translating the master plan's vision into detailed architectural designs for key residential and commercial blocks, ensuring a cohesive aesthetic across the mixed-use development.
  • Material & Systems Innovation: Led the research and specification of innovative facade materials and systems that balanced aesthetic intent with performance requirements for solar shading, ventilation, and durability.
  • Stakeholder & Consultant Coordination: Managed the seamless coordination between international design consultants, local engineering teams, and the client to ensure the design integrity was maintained through to execution.
  • Architectural Detailing & Documentation: Oversaw the production of comprehensive architectural drawings and specifications for the building envelope, focusing on precision and buildability to achieve a high-quality finish.
