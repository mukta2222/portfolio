Cottage in Manali

Manali, Himachal Pradesh

Project Highlights

Contextual Modernism

A design that blends modern architectural principles with traditional Himachali aesthetics, featuring sloping roofs and the use of local wood and stone to harmonize with the mountainous landscape.

Integration with Nature

Expansive windows and balconies are strategically placed to frame stunning views of the surrounding valleys and forests, blurring the lines between indoor and outdoor spaces.

Sustainable Solutions

Incorporates passive solar design for natural heating during cold winters and uses locally sourced, sustainable materials to minimize environmental impact.

Functional Elegance

The interior layout is optimized for tourist accommodation, offering a blend of luxury, comfort, and practicality with open-plan living areas and private, cozy bedrooms.

My Contributions

Design Conceptualization & Development

Led the project from the initial concept phase, developing a design narrative that merged the client's vision for a modern tourist unit with the rich architectural heritage of Manali. This involved creating the spatial hierarchy and material palette.

Project Management & Coordination

Oversaw the entire project lifecycle, ensuring seamless coordination between structural engineers, local contractors, and material suppliers to maintain design integrity and adherence to timelines.

Innovative Material Application

Championed the use of contemporary finishes alongside traditional materials. This included specifying high-performance glazing for thermal comfort and designing bespoke wooden ceiling patterns that reflect local craftsmanship.
